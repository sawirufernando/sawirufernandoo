Magnificent Wildlife

Being a tropical island, Sri Lanka boasts a high number of endemic species, animals and plants alike. The sheer number of species that call this little island their home as led Sri Lanka to be regarded as one of the top five hotspots for biodiversity.

Whether you are high up in the central highlands or in the dense rainforests you're bound to encounter some of the rarest and amazing animals which you've probably never seen before. Don't forget to take a look at the Yala, Udawalawa and Minneriya National parks where you will see species such as-

  • Leopards
  • Elephants
  • Spotted deer
  • Sloth Bears


Spectacular Monuments

Sri Lanka has a long and wondrous history which spans thousands of years, from the arrival of king Vijaya from India to the reign of local kings, Portugese settlement, Dutch settlement all the way until the rule of the British Imperial Crown. All these eras under diverse leaderships and influences has led Sri Lanka to host a marvelous number of ancient buildings and sites which you can witness in almost any part of the country

Being a strong Buddhist nation for hundreds of years, this country also has many temples to which hundreds of thousands of pilgrims pay homage each year. If you visit the northern parts of the country, you will also be able to witness many Hindu spiritual sites as well. Here are some of the most significant places with historical value-

  1. Sigiriya
  2. Anuradhapura
  3. Polonnaruwa
  4. Galle Fort


Breathtaking Sceneries

Dotted with high mountains, romantic landscapes and golden beaches, this little island nation has no shortage of breathtaking sceneries. Two seasons of monsoon rain every year guarantees that almost every part of the country remains lush and beautiful throughout the year. You can visit most parts of the island by train, which means you get to see nature's beauty as you get there too!

Don't forget to explore the countryside charm of Ella, the bellows of blue whales in Mirissa, the lush greenery of Nuwara Eliya, the surreal village of Kandalama, the wondrous view of nine arch bridge, the pristine beaches of Arugam Bay and the picturesque beauty of the old Dutch fort in Galle.
