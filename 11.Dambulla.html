Awe-Inspiring Cave Temple

This awe-inpiring UNESCO world heritage site is situated towering above the vast plains of Dambulla. This is in fact the largest and best preserved cave temple complex in Sri Lanka. It consists of five major caves which are lined with more than a hundered Buddha statues with few depicitng Sri Lankan Kings, gods and goddesses.

The Cave Temple premises are open to visitors from 7AM til 7PM, and please note that shoulders and knees must be covered in a decent manner and hats must be taken off as This is a site with not only historical value, but immense spiritual value as well.

Gorgeous Jathika Namal Uyana

The largest ironwood as well as pink quartz mountain in Sri Lanka, the Jathika Namal Uyana is situated 7km from the Madatugama junction of Colombo-Anuradhapura highway. It is a certainely a unique location in the country as ironwood trees dominate the vegetation of around 250 acres of forest reserve. This was a sanctuary for Buddhist monks and was later used as a prison camp, where the punishment was quite interestingly, to plant Na trees. The Na tree is also regarded as the national tree of Sri Lanka.

According to botanists, the importance of Namal Uyana lies in its 102 species of tropical plants of which 82 are herbal plants within 250 acres of graden and the forest sorrounding it. It is also home to 18 species of birds and a number of mammals too. The cool breeze, fresh air and greenery along with the history associated with this place certainely puts it in a traveler's bucket list of things to do in Sri Lanka.

Ibbankatuwa Megalithic Tombs

Not as famous as you would expect it to be, but nonethless of great archiological value, this megalithic burial site dates all the way back to the iron age. A quick visit here is sure to give you and your curious kids a good understanding of what the lifestyle of Sri Lankans who inhabited this land eons ago was like. This burial site which is very well maintained also houses a museum where you will find clay pots, copper and gold jewellry, urns and gemstones belonging to this land's ancestors.

This ancient burial site which belongs to the megalithic prehistoric and proto historic period of Sri Lanka is situated just about five kilometres off Dambulla town, and is considered to be one of the oldest of several burial sites that have been found in the island. It is open to visitors from 9AM to 6PM on weekdays and 9AM to 5PM on weekends.
